Our Top Picks

Confessions of Nairobi Women

Joan Thatiah

Confessions of Nairobi Women by Joan Thatiah is a gripping collection of thirteen true stories that expose the raw, messy, and awe-inspiring realities of women living in Nairobi.

It’s a bold, unfiltered look into lives shaped by trauma, resilience, and the relentless hustle of city life. From tales of love, betrayal, addiction, and survival, each confession reveals the emotional and societal battles women face—often with heartbreaking vulnerability and fierce courage.

The Art of War

Sun Tzu

The Art of War is Sun Tzu’s timeless guide to strategy, deception, and discipline in warfare.

Written in ancient China, it teaches how to win battles through planning, adaptability, and psychological insight—often without fighting. Its 13 chapters offer lessons that extend beyond war, influencing leadership, business, and personal growth—with wisdom still relevant today.

Finding Me

Viola Davis

A fierce story of resilience and self-discovery

Finding Me is Viola Davis’s raw memoir of surviving poverty, abuse, and racism to become a celebrated actor. Acting becomes her escape and purpose. She battles colorism and rejection, finds healing through therapy and love, and earns the Triple Crown of Acting.

Let Me Call You Back

Jackson Biko

Let Me Call You Back by Jackson Biko is a witty, emotionally charged novel about Samora, a Nairobi man whose life unravels after losing his job. As he hides the truth from his family, the weight of masculinity, societal expectations, and personal pride push him deeper into isolation. Through therapy, heartbreak, and self-reflection, Samora’s journey reveals the quiet struggles of modern Kenyan men—where silence can be more destructive than failure. It’s raw, humorous, and deeply human.

Stay with Me

Ayobami Adebayo

Stay With Me by Ayọ̀bámi Adébáyọ̀ is a heart-wrenching novel set in Nigeria, following Yejide and Akin, a couple struggling with infertility and societal pressure. When Akin’s family introduces a second wife, their marriage spirals into secrecy, betrayal, and grief. Through alternating perspectives, the story explores love, motherhood, and resilience amid cultural expectations and personal tragedy.

Science Fiction

We Are All Birds of Uganda

Hafsa Zayyan

We Are All Birds of Uganda by Hafsa Zayyan is a poignant dual-narrative novel that spans generations and continents. It follows Sameer, a successful lawyer in London, and Hasan, his grandfather in 1960s Uganda, as they grapple with identity, belonging, and displacement. Through letters and personal journeys, the story explores the legacy of colonialism, racial tension, and the search for home.

The Sweetness of Water

Nathan Harris

The Sweetness of Water by Nathan Harris is a deeply moving historical novel set in post–Civil War Georgia. It follows two newly freed brothers, Prentiss and Landry, who find refuge on the farm of George Walker, a grieving white landowner. As unlikely bonds form, the town’s racial tensions and secrets—including a forbidden romance between two Confederate soldiers—ignite conflict and tragedy. Through lyrical prose and layered characters, the novel explores freedom, grief, love, and the fragile hope of reconciliation.

Womb City

Tlotlo Tsamaase

Womb City by Tlotlo Tsamaase is a genre-bending Afrofuturist horror novel set in a dystopian, surveillance-heavy Botswana. Architect Nelah lives in a body controlled by microchip, trapped in a loveless marriage, and haunted by a crime she committed. When the ghost of her victim begins a bloody revenge, Nelah must unravel a political conspiracy and confront the monstrous systems that govern her world. It’s a chilling tale of body autonomy, motherhood, and rebellion—blending cyberpunk, folklore, and psychological horror.

Fantasy

Somadina

Akwaeke Emezi

Somadina by Akwaeke Emezi is a spellbinding young adult fantasy set in a magical West African world. When her twin brother Jayaike vanishes, Somadina—whose powers evoke fear—must journey through the Sacred Forest and confront hostile forces to save him. It’s a tale of identity, courage, and ancestral magic woven with emotional depth and cultural richness.

Ice Breaker

Hannah Grace

Icebreaker by Hannah Grace is a spicy sports romance set at the University of California, Maple Hills. Figure skater Anastasia Allen is laser-focused on making Team USA—until a rink mishap forces her to share ice time with Nate Hawkins, the charming hockey captain she can’t stand. Sparks fly, tension builds, and when her skating partner gets injured, Nate becomes her unexpected ally. As they train together, their rivalry melts into attraction, challenging Anastasia’s guarded heart and redefining her idea of love, trust, and ambition. It’s witty, emotional, and full of found-family warmth.

The Night We Got Stuck In A Story

Ben Miller

The Night We Got Stuck in a Story is a magical adventure by Ben Miller featuring siblings Lana and Harrison. While staying with their grandparents, they discover a hollow tree that transports them into a storybook world. When Harrison is kidnapped by the evil Spider Queen, Lana must brave enchanted lands, save the villagers, and restore balance before dawn.

Decolonising The Mind: Politics of Language

Ngugi wa Thiong'o

Decolonising the Mind is Ngũgĩ wa Thiong’o’s powerful manifesto on language and liberation. Through essays, he argues that colonial powers used language to suppress African identity and culture. He calls for writers to embrace indigenous languages as tools of resistance, reclaiming African literature, memory, and dignity from imperial domination.

Why Nations Fail

Daron Acemoglu & James A Robinson

Why Nations Fail explores why some countries thrive while others remain trapped in poverty. Acemoglu and Robinson argue that inclusive political and economic institutions—those that empower broad participation—lead to prosperity. In contrast, extractive institutions—controlled by elites—stifle innovation and growth. Through vivid historical examples, the book reveals how power shapes progress.

Against The Tide

Martha Karua

Against the Tide is Martha Karua’s inspiring memoir chronicling her rise from a village in Kirinyaga to Kenya’s political frontlines. With fierce integrity, she battles injustice, defends human rights, and challenges stereotypes. It’s a bold reflection on leadership, resilience, and the power of staying true to one’s convictions—while embracing vulnerability and joy.

The Autobiography of Malcolm X

Malcolm X and Alex Haley

The Autobiography of Malcolm X traces the journey of Malcolm X from a troubled youth to a powerful civil rights leader. Through prison, religious awakening, and global travels, Malcolm X evolves from a militant separatist to a voice for universal human rights. It’s a raw, transformative story of identity and purpose.
